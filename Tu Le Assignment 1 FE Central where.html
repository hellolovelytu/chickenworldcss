Where to buy

Big stores

If you want to hand-pick your chick or hatching eggs, there are some chain stores you can visit. Boomgaar and Tractor Supply often have their "Chick days" from March until July.

Hatcheries

Some hatcheries are allowed people to come and pick up their chicks. Most of them will ship your chickens and hatching eggs through US Postal Services. Common hatcheries are Meyer, Cackle, McMurray, Stromberg and Valley. You can look up which hatcheries are near your location to reduce shipping and handling fee. They can ship year round.

Local farms and ranches

Often time, local farms and ranches offer hatching eggs and live chicks too. You can join a local social media group to find out who is offering which varieties.

Pros and cons of each option

Where Pros Cons
Big stores Can hand-pick chickens, inspect their health and sex, lower cost, can mix match varieties Not available year round, not many varieties to choose from, no hatching eggs offer, minimum order range from 3 to 10 chickens
Hatcheries Can hand-pick chickens if you live near by, more varieties to choose from, available year round, have hatching eggs, offer vaccination Many fees to pay (shipping, handling, small order, etc.), may end up with extra unwanted chickens, minimum order is applied to each variety, chicks may be stressed from long transportation
Farms and ranches Lower cost, more options to choose (from varieties to age), offer hatching eggs, ensure that the specific variety can thrive in your climate, available more often than big stores Pick up drive can be long, could be scammed, no vaccination offer or guarantee on health condition
